# Cold Call Framework — B2B SDR Reference

**Core principle:** Never argue. Never push. State shared realities. Ask if they match.

---

## The 3 Shared Realities

Build every call around establishing these before any mention of your product:

1. **Reality 1: Their world has a specific challenge** — something is growing harder, slower, or more expensive
2. **Reality 2: The status quo has a cost** — staying with the current approach has a real price (time, money, risk)
3. **Reality 3: Others like them have solved it** — a comparable company achieved [metric] by addressing this

---

## The 30-Second Opener Structure

### 1. Pattern Interrupt (First 3 seconds)
**Goal:** Stop the "it's a sales call, hang up" reflex.

**What works:**
- Honesty: "I'll be honest — this is a cold call." [pause] "Have I caught you at a terrible time?"
- Specificity: "I'm calling specifically because [research signal]." (shows you did your homework)

**The permission question:** "Have I caught you at a terrible time?"
- If yes → "Fair — can I have 20 seconds to tell you why I called? You can tell me if there's something here."
- If no → Go to Bridge immediately. Don't waste the window.

---

### 2. Bridge (5 seconds): Why You, Why Today

**Pattern:** "The reason I'm calling specifically is [hot signal from research]."

**Examples (fill in with your research):**
- "[Company] just announced [product launch] — every new product surface usually comes with a [relevant data/infra/process] decision."
- "I saw you're hiring [X engineers] — that usually signals a new initiative is coming."
- "[Company] just closed a [funding round] — congratulations. We work with teams right after raises because that's when the next scaling question surfaces."
- "Your team just moved [project] to production — that's exactly when teams hit [relevant pain]."

**Rule:** The bridge must be specific and research-confirmed. Generic signals get hung up on.

---

### 3. Reason for Call (10 seconds): 1 Pain + 1 Outcome

**Pattern:** "We work with [type of team] who [their pain]. They [outcome with metric]."

**Rules:**
- 1 pain only. Not 3.
- 1 outcome only. Not a list.
- Never mention product features. Only outcomes.

---

### 4. The Soft Ask (5 seconds)

**Pattern:** "I'm not trying to sell you anything today — I wanted to see if it's worth 20 minutes with {{AE_NAME}}, who works with [their type of] teams."

**Variations:**
- "Would [day] or [day] work for a 20-minute call?"
- "Can I send you one thing — a 2-minute case study from [comparable company] — and we reconnect next week?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be worth 20 minutes?" (if skeptical but not hanging up)

---

## AAA Objection Handling

**A — Acknowledge** (always first — never argue or talk over them)
**A — Ask** (reframe with a question to understand root cause)
**A — Advance** (earn the next 30 seconds toward a yes or a useful no)

### Common Objections

**"We're not interested."**
- Acknowledge: "That makes sense — most teams aren't until they see [specific thing]."
- Ask: "Can I ask one quick question — is that because you're locked into something, or because [relevant topic] just isn't a priority right now?"
- Advance: "Because if it's the latter, I have something specific to [company] that might change that. Would 20 seconds do it?"

**"We're happy with what we have."**
- Acknowledge: "That's actually the most common thing I hear before [common pain] surfaces."
- Ask: "What does [their process] look like today — at your scale?"
- Advance: "The reason I ask is that [comparable company] said the same thing — until [specific event]."

**"Send me an email."**
- Don't just say "sure" and hang up. Use it.
- "Happy to. What should I put in the subject line so it doesn't get buried? What are you focused on right now?"
- THEN send the email SAME DAY with that exact context.

**"No budget."**
- Acknowledge: "Budget's not what I'm asking for today."
- Ask: "Is this a timing question or a priority question?"
- Advance: "If it's timing — what quarter makes more sense? I'll follow up then."

**"We already evaluated you / looked at this."**
- Acknowledge: "Good to know — what was the conclusion?"
- Ask: "Was that for [specific use case]? The reason I'm calling now is [new capability / new angle]."
- Advance: "If the evaluation was more than [X months] ago, it might be worth a 20-minute update."

**"Talk to my manager / team lead."**
- "Happy to — what's the best way to reach them? And can I mention your name?"

## Voicemail (20-30 seconds max)

```
"[Name], this is [your name] at {{COMPANY_NAME}}.
Quick question about [hot signal — specific to their company].
Best number is [your number]. I'll also send an email."
```

**Rules:**
- Always say you'll follow up with email (they check email more)
- Reference something specific to their company (not a generic pitch)
- End with 1 hook, not a sales pitch

---

## Call Flow Cheat Sheet

```
1. Pattern interrupt: "I'll be honest — cold call. Terrible time?"
   YES → "20 seconds, then you decide"
   NO  → Bridge immediately

2. Bridge: "Calling because [hot signal from research]"

3. Pain + outcome: "Teams like [type] who [pain] → [outcome with metric]"

4. Soft ask: "20 min with {{AE_NAME}} this week?"
   YES → Book it
   NO  → Objection handling (AAA)

5. Objection handled → Back to soft ask OR
   "Can I send one thing and reconnect [specific day]?"
```

## Pre-Call Prep Checklist

- [ ] Know the hot signal you'll open with (research-confirmed)
- [ ] Know the pain for their specific role (from buyer-personas.md)
- [ ] Know the proof point you'll use if asked for evidence
- [ ] Have {{AE_NAME}}'s name + title ready
- [ ] Have 2 specific dates/times ready for the soft ask
- [ ] Voicemail message mentally rehearsed
- [ ] Email drafted and ready to send within 10 min of leaving voicemail

## What Happened (1-paragraph summary)

Intro call with Phindile Khumalo, the first-ever reward person at Unitrans/KAP. She described a deeply manual, payroll-owned process where 9 executives manage increases for ~7,000 employees with no performance data, no pay scales, and no audit trail. Increases have historically been flat % across the board, with managers redistributing based on personal preference ("rob Paul to pay Peter"). Ricky demoed salary increases, org structure, eligibility rules, letters, and briefly touched on performance management. Phindile was positive -- said "this is exactly something that would be assisting me" -- but the Alignd side spoke too much, demo was broad rather than targeted, and the explicit link between her pains and Alignd's value (governance, visibility, policy alignment) wasn't made tightly enough. Phindile committed to an internal meeting next Tuesday and will come back with a date for a broader executive demo.

## Their World
- **Cycle timing:** Financial year July-June. Increases effective 1 July. Performance management just started last year -- cycle timing mismatch means ratings won't be ready for July increases. Phindile exploring backdating increases to September to allow proper ratings.
- **Platforms:** Sage (payroll + failing attempt at performance management). No dedicated reward platform. No pay scales. Excel spreadsheets sent to 9 executives who manage ~7,000 non-bargaining unit employees across SA + African countries. Bargaining unit is separate.
- **Org structure:** Phindile is the first reward person ever hired at Unitrans or KAP. No reward function existed before her. HR business partners are only being introduced to the increase process this year for the first time. OD/Effectiveness person (Faith) started July 2025 and is responsible for performance management. Process currently owned by payroll, not HR.

## Challenges
1. **9 executives managing 7,000 people with no data** -- flat % increases, managers "rob Paul to pay Peter" based on who they like, no performance ratings, no evidence base. Phindile: "I'm just because Peter greeted me nicely today because there's no performance management in place."
2. **No performance management until this year** -- literally the first year implementing it. Goal setting still being begged for in March. 2026 is a "trial year."
3. **No pay scales** -- "We didn't have pay scales here, so we were paying whatever... nothing was standardized." HR people picking different market percentiles with no consistency.
4. **Disability employees getting increases by mistake** -- people on disability still on Unitrans payroll, receiving increases they shouldn't due to Sage process errors.
5. **4+ retirement funds at Unitrans level alone** (SA only) + different funds per African country -- consolidation complexity.
6. **Sage is a payroll system, not a people system** -- Phindile's words. Being used for performance management and failing.
7. **Cycle timing mismatch** -- increases effective July 1, but performance ratings won't be ready. Exploring backdating to September.
8. **No post-review reporting** -- "there's no post review reports to say these were the findings, these were the mistakes, this is where they happened."
9. **Process owned by payroll, not HR** -- Phindile wants HR to own end-to-end.
10. **No visibility for leadership** -- before Phindile, nobody was asking the right questions. "They didn't know what they didn't know."
11. **Multi-country currency handling** -- currently convert to ZAR, apply increase, convert back. All countries get the same mandate % regardless of local inflation. No local market data.

## Deeper Pain
- **Governance link:** Partially made. The audit trail, eligibility rules, and structured approvals were shown in the demo but not explicitly connected back to Phindile's specific governance gaps (disability errors, no post-review reporting, no evidence for increase decisions).
- **Visibility link:** Not effectively made. Dashboard and real-time budget tracking were shown but not framed as solving her specific "I can't report to leadership" problem.
- **Fair pay / Policy alignment:** Lightly touched. The comp ratio and performance matrix were shown, which directly address the "rob Paul to pay Peter" problem and lack of pay scales. But the connection wasn't made explicitly enough.
- **Biggest missed opportunity:** Phindile described a world where 9 people decide increases for 7,000 with zero data. That's a governance crisis. We showed the tools that fix it but didn't name the risk loudly enough.

## 4. Commercial Insight (Challenger -- Teach)

**Adapt for a practitioner audience.** Don't lecture. Share as a peer observation.

**The insight (delivered conversationally, not as a presentation):**
> "One thing we see with reward teams managing multi-country operations -- the biggest risk isn't getting payroll wrong. It's the gap between what your reward policy says and what actually happens in the cycle. Payroll tells you what was paid. But can you show that every decision was within policy, equitable across geographies, and would hold up if a union rep or the Top Employers auditor asked? That governance layer between policy and payment is where most teams have a blind spot -- and it's usually because the tools don't support it."

**Why this works for Phindile specifically:**
She's the one who lives in that gap. She knows the pain of consolidating spreadsheets, chasing approvals, and hoping the numbers are right. This insight validates her experience and gives her language to describe the problem to leadership.

## 5. Call Script Framework

### A) Warm-Up (5 mins) -- Dena leads

- Dena opens: Personal catch-up with Phindile. "Great to reconnect." Keep it genuine and brief.
- Josh: "Phindile, thanks for reaching out after seeing Neil's post. Before we dive in, I'd love to understand what caught your eye -- what resonated about what Neil shared?"
- This opening does two things: (1) lets Phindile tell you why she's here, (2) reveals what she's already thinking about.

### B) Discovery Questions (20 mins) -- Josh leads, Dena follows up

**Start with her world, not the company:**

#### Her Role + Daily Reality
1. "Tell me about your role at Unitrans. What does a typical week look like for you in reward and benefits?"
2. "How big is the reward team? Is it just you, or is there a broader team across countries?"
3. "What's your remit -- SA only, or do you cover all 10 countries?"

#### Current Process (the gold)
4. "Walk me through your last salary review cycle. How did it actually work -- from data gathering to final approval?"
5. "What tools are you using day-to-day? Excel, SAP, something else?"
6. "How do you handle benchmarking -- what data sources do you use?"
7. "How do increases and bonuses get approved? What does the approval chain look like?"
8. "How long does a full cycle take end to end?"

#### Where It Breaks (constructive tension -- Dena can add here)
9. "Where in that process do you lose the most time?"
10. "What's the most frustrating part of your current setup?"
11. "How do you handle the multi-country piece -- different currencies, labour laws, bargaining councils?"
12. "When leadership asks for a quick view on pay equity or total reward spend -- how easy is that to produce?"
13. "How confident are you in the audit trail from policy to payment?"

#### Aspirational (what does good look like?)
14. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how Unitrans manages reward -- what would it be?"
15. "What does 'good' look like for you? What would make you feel like you've got proper control?"

### C) Alignd Walkthrough (15 mins) -- Ricky leads, Dena translates

**Only show what maps to what Phindile said.** Do NOT feature dump.

| If Phindile says... | Ricky shows... | Dena says... |
|---------------------|----------------|--------------|
| "We use Excel for everything" | Unified reward cycle workspace | "This is what replaced spreadsheets for [pattern] -- the cycle lives in one place" |
| "Multi-country is a nightmare" | Multi-geography view, currency handling, regulatory frameworks | "The beauty is each country has its own policy rules built in" |
| "Approvals take forever" | Structured approval workflows, routing | "Managers see exactly what they need to approve and the guardrails prevent out-of-policy decisions" |
| "I can't get visibility for leadership" | Dashboards, real-time budget tracking | "This is what your Head of HC or CFO would see without asking you to build a report" |
| "Benchmarking is manual" | Market data integration, live feeds | "Alignd pulls market data in so you're benchmarking in real-time, not once a year" |
| "Union negotiations are stressful" | Equity reporting, policy compliance checks | "You can show any union rep exactly how decisions were made -- transparent, defensible" |

**Key framing for a practitioner:**
- "This is what your day-to-day would look like in Alignd"
- "This replaces the spreadsheet step you described"
- "This is the view you'd send to Zene / leadership instead of building it manually"

### D) Business Case + Next Steps (10 mins) -- Josh leads

**Arm Phindile to sell internally:**

1. "Based on what you've seen -- does this feel like it addresses the challenges you described?"
2. "If this is something you wanted to explore further, who else at Unitrans would need to be part of the conversation?"
3. "Would Zene Olivier need to see this? What about someone from IT or finance?"
4. "What would Zene care about most -- governance, visibility, efficiency, or something else?"
5. "How do decisions like this typically get made at Unitrans? Is there a procurement process?"
6. "Would it be helpful if we put together a short summary you could share internally -- a one-pager on what Alignd does and why it's relevant for Unitrans specifically?"
